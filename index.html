Safety & Privacy

A wellbeing product must earn trust first.

Sarthya is built as a supportive reflection companion, not a medical or crisis service. We are transparent about what it can and cannot do.

Not a replacement for therapy
Sarthya can support everyday reflection, stress, and emotional clarity, but it does not diagnose, prescribe, or replace licensed care.
Private by design
The current demo does not require sign-up. As accounts are added, user control, consent, and data minimization will remain core principles.
Crisis-aware
If someone may be in immediate danger, Sarthya guides them toward urgent human support and crisis resources instead of pretending to solve it alone.
FAQ

Questions we hear often.

Is Sarthya a replacement for therapy? +
No — Sarthya is a supportive companion, not a clinical tool. For serious mental health concerns, we always encourage connecting with our human counselors or a licensed professional. Sarthya is best for everyday emotional support, reflection, and building healthier habits.
Is my data private and confidential? +
The current demo is designed to be low-friction and does not require account creation. As Sarthya grows, privacy, consent, and user control will remain central. Please avoid sharing highly sensitive personal details in the demo version.
How are counselors vetted? +
The therapist network is planned as the next phase. Counselors will be onboarded only after credential checks, clear service guidelines, and a safe support process are in place.
Can I use Sarthya anonymously? +
Yes. You can start conversations without creating an account. If you choose to sign up, your display name can be anything you're comfortable with — you don't need to share your real identity.
What languages does Sarthya support? +
Sarthya currently focuses on English and simple Hindi-style communication. More Indian languages can be added as user demand becomes clearer.
What if I'm in a crisis or emergency? +
If you are in immediate danger, contact local emergency services or someone near you right now. In India, you can also contact iCall at 9152987821 or Vandrevala Foundation at 1860-2662-345. Sarthya is not an emergency service.
